## Executive Summary

Southeast Wisconsin's private school market offers **$2-3M+ annual painting opportunity** with characteristics perfectly aligned with LaBrant's capabilities:

- **100+ private/parochial schools** in 10-county area
- **Summer scheduling window** (June-August) matches painting contractor capacity
- **Lead certification REQUIRED** for most buildings (pre-1978 construction)
- **Decentralized procurement** - principals/facility managers decide
- **Repeat business** - 5-7 year classroom refresh cycles
- **NON-UNION environment** - private schools don't require union labor

**Critical Timing:** Summer 2026 school painting must be **bid by March-April 2026**. Start outreach NOW.

## Lead Certification Requirements

### Regulatory Framework

**EPA RRP Rule + Wisconsin DHS:**
- Applies to pre-1978 buildings
- Schools are "child-occupied facilities" (stricter requirements)
- Certified renovator must be on-site
- Lead-safe work practices mandatory
- Documentation and recordkeeping required

### School Building Age Analysis

| Era | % of Schools | Lead Risk | Certification Need |
|-----|--------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Pre-1950 | ~30% | HIGH | **REQUIRED** |
| 1950-1978 | ~40% | MODERATE | **REQUIRED** |
| Post-1978 | ~30% | LOW | Not required |

**Result:** ~70% of private school buildings require lead-certified contractors

### Marketing Lead Certification

**To School Administrators:**

> "Federal EPA regulations require certified contractors for any renovation work in schools built before 1978. As a Wisconsin DHS certified lead renovator, we ensure your project complies with all regulations while protecting students and staff from lead exposure.
>
> Our lead-safe work practices include proper containment, specialized cleaning, and documentation--giving you peace of mind and regulatory compliance."

## Summer Scheduling Strategy

### Capacity Planning

**Summer 2026 Target:**
- Available window: June 10 - August 20 (10 weeks)
- Working days: ~50 days
- Crew capacity needed: Plan for 2-3 simultaneous school projects

### Project Sequencing

| Week | Project Type | Crew Allocation |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1-2 | Large classroom building | Full crew |
| 3-4 | Continue + start second school | Split crews |
| 5-6 | Complete first, focus second | Rotate |
| 7-8 | Third school + touch-ups | Full crew |
| 9-10 | Final completion + punch lists | All hands |

- Small local painters (often not lead-certified)
- General maintenance contractors
- Larger commercial firms (for big projects)
- In-house maintenance (touch-up only)

### Competitive Advantages

| Factor | LaBrant Advantage |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Lead Certification** | Many competitors lack this |
| **Commercial Scale** | Can handle large school projects |
| **Summer Capacity** | Dedicated crew availability |
| **Quality Focus** | Not lowest-bid positioning |
| **Local Presence** | Relationship accessibility |

### Differentiation Strategy

**Position as "School Painting Specialist":**
- Create school-specific capability statement
- Collect school references and photos
- Develop school-specific pricing packages

## Action Plan

### Phase 1: Research & Preparation (January 2026)

1. **Build Target List**
   - Create spreadsheet of 50 target schools
   - Include: Name, address, contact, building age, last painted
   - Prioritize by: Size, building age, location

2. **Prepare Materials**
   - School-specific capability statement
   - Lead certification documentation
   - Sample proposals
   - Reference list

### Phase 2: Outreach (February-March 2026)

3. **Initial Contact Campaign**
   - Week 1-2: Email to all 50 targets
   - Week 3-4: Phone follow-up
   - Week 5-6: Schedule facility visits

4. **Facility Assessments**
   - Free assessments for interested schools
   - Document scope, building age, condition
   - Identify lead paint concerns
   - Prepare detailed proposals

### Phase 3: Proposal & Close (March-April 2026)

5. **Submit Proposals**
   - Detailed scope and pricing
   - Lead-safe work plan (if applicable)
   - Schedule and timeline
   - References from similar projects

6. **Follow-Up & Negotiate**
   - Weekly check-ins on pending proposals
   - Address questions promptly
   - Negotiate scope/phasing if needed

### Phase 4: Execute (June-August 2026)

7. **Summer Work**
   - Mobilize immediately when school ends
   - Daily communication with school contact
   - Quality checkpoints throughout
   - Complete before school starts

8. **Post-Project**
   - Walk-through with school representative
   - Collect testimonial/reference
   - Ask for referrals to other schools
   - Schedule touch-up for following summer
